Three Suggested Cases:
BookMyShow, Nykaa, and Myntra.
BookMyShow: It dominates India's online ticketing space with a 60%–80% market share. Its nearest competitor is Paytm, with a 15%–20% share.
Nykaa: Holds a 35%–40% share of India’s online Beauty and Personal Care market. Competitors include Amazon (20–25%), Flipkart (10–15%), and Purplle (5–10%).
Myntra: With over 50% market share, it is the undisputed leader in digital fashion. Reliance’s AJIO is rapidly catching up with 25%–30% share.

Titan – Case Interstitial

When Titan entered India’s watch market, the category was defined by the state-run incumbent (HMT) and a low-innovation competitor (Allwyn). The dominant logic was functional: accurate timekeeping at accessible price points. Titan reframed the category by foregrounding design, aspiration, and lifestyle association — elevating a utilitarian product into a personal identity marker.

Tip: Please, pause at key timestamps, and think - why, how, what?
Compare “fight the leader” vs category creation.

Tata Tea – Case Interstitial

In the late 1980s and early 1990s, Hindustan Unilever dominated packaged tea with Red Label and Taj Mahal. Tata Tea, then a plantation-driven company, recognised a structural opening: consumer dissatisfaction with generic blends and a growing appetite for regional taste authenticity. The rest is history!

Tip: Please, pause at key timestamps, and think - why, how, what?
Compare “fight the leader” vs category creation.

Tanisq – Case Interstitial

Tanishq’s early years were marked by strategic misalignment: Western design sensibilities and a premium retail format that did not resonate with Indian jewellery’s deep cultural and emotional anchors. A seven-year insight programme revealed that consumers valued purity assurance, ritual alignment, and local aesthetic codes far more than global trends.

Tip: Please, pause at key timestamps, and think - why, how, what?
Focus on belief change, by institutionalising trust.
